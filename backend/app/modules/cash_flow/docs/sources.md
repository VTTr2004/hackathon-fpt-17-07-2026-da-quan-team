# Sources

Accessed `2026-07-18`.

1. IFRS Foundation, **IAS 7 Statement of Cash Flows**. https://www.ifrs.org/issued-standards/list-of-standards/ias-7-statement-of-cash-flows.html/
   - Used for cash/cash-equivalent scope and operating, investing and financing classification.
   - High reliability; accounting-standard authority. The MVP is analytical software, not an IFRS compliance opinion.
2. OpenStax, **Principles of Accounting, Volume 2 — Calculate a Break-Even Point in Units and Dollars**. https://openstax.org/books/principles-managerial-accounting/pages/3-2-calculate-a-break-even-point-in-units-and-dollars
   - Used for contribution margin and break-even revenue methodology.
   - High reliability for educational methodology; application still requires confirmed fixed/variable cost inputs.
3. Verlyn D. Richards and Eugene J. Laughlin (1980), **A Cash Conversion Cycle Approach to Liquidity Analysis**, *Financial Management*, DOI `10.2307/3665310`.
   - Used for the cash conversion cycle framing linking receivables, inventory and payables.
   - Peer-reviewed foundational source; exact inputs and industry interpretation remain context-dependent.
4. Internal product specification, `plans/trung-plans.md`, Module 2.
   - Defines ownership, minimum tools, no-LLM-arithmetic rule, output and acceptance criteria for this repository.

Research and standards define method; startup conclusions must still come from startup documents, confirmed facts or calculation outputs.
